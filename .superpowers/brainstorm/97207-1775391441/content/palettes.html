1. Warm Stoneware

Terracotta and linen. The ceramic in the art bookshop.

Warm off-white, earthy terracotta/sienna accents, Charter serif kept. Gentle gradient accent bar. Feels handmade, tactile, grounded. PDIA images sit naturally alongside like illustrations in a printed journal.

LRB meets personal blog. Navy, authority, whitespace.

3. The Broadsheet

Baffler energy. Big type, tight columns, one red accent.

Near-white background, black text, single red (#c44) for section labels. Heavy 900-weight headlines, double-rule borders, three-column grid. Newspaper energy - playful, confident typography. The most fun option. PDIA images run large as feature illustrations.

4. Soft Archive

Sage and stone. A curated collection in a quiet room.

Warm grey (#f2efe8) background, sage green (#6b8f71) accents, muted earth tones. PDIA images are prominent, displayed alongside content like illustrations in a natural history book. Library/archive energy. Calm, considered, collected.

5. Chromatic Studio

Each section gets its own colour. A unified rainbow.

Near-white background, each content type has a signature colour (navy for writing, amber for photos, green for library, plum for links). Colour-coded pills in nav and inline. The most modern/web-native option. Homepage is a mixed feed with colour cues showing content type at a glance.
