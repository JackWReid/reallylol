Library — 4 ways to make text-only books interesting

No covers. Just title, author, date. 1400 books need to be browsable and not boring.

A: Timeline — Grouped by when you read them

Time becomes the organising principle. Big year markers, months as subheads. You're reading your own reading history. Emphasises the rhythm and volume of what you consume.

B: The Index — Author-grouped, dense, browsable

Alphabetical by author. Like the back of a really good bookshop. You can scan fast because the structure is predictable. Multiple books by the same author collapse together. Feels like an index in a reference book.

C: Stacked Spines — Visual rhythm from typography alone

Each book is a horizontal bar, like a spine on a shelf seen from the side. Varying widths based on... nothing, just random. The visual interest comes from the density and the alternating treatment of title (Fraunces) and author (Plex). Feels physical.

D: The Shelf — Timeline view, but with visual weight

Combine A's chronological grouping with generated colour blocks per book. Each book gets a coloured rectangle (derived from the title hash, or just cycling through the palette). Not a cover, but not nothing. Gives the page visual rhythm.
