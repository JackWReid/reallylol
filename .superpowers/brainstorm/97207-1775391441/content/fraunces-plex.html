On walking through Ludlow in February

The high street curves and you lose sight of the castle for a moment. There's a butcher with hand-lettered signs and a bookshop that smells like it should. Ordinary Notes sits in my coat pocket, spine already cracked.

You walk past the church and the light changes. Something about the angle of the street funnels it. The stone goes from grey to almost gold, and you understand why people have been living here for a thousand years.

To annotate is to add a note, a comment, an explanation. But it is also to notice.

The castle is open but empty. February in a market town means you have the place to yourself. The woman at the ticket desk looks surprised to see anyone at all.
