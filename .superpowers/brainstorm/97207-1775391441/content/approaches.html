A: Refined Editorial

Keep the warmth, fix the structure. Same cream palette, Charter serif, gold accents. But: flatten navigation to a single clean bar, replace pagination with infinite scroll or "load more", give photos a proper masonry grid, consolidate links/highlights/blogroll into one "Links" section. PDIA images as section headers and decorative breaks.

Pros

  • Lowest risk - familiar but better
  • Fixes functional issues without identity crisis
  • PDIA images slot in naturally

Cons

  • Might still feel "same" after the work
  • Doesn't address the per-section personality you liked about Notes

B: Zoned Personalities

Each section has its own visual world. Same base palette, but Writing gets the literary serif treatment, Photos get a dark/minimal gallery feel, Library (books+films merged) gets a cosy catalogue grid, Links gets a compact dense layout. Sections feel like rooms in the same house. Homepage becomes a feed mixing recent items from all zones. PDIA images as per-zone decorative motifs.

Pros

  • Extends the Notes differentiation you love to every section
  • Makes each content type shine on its own terms
  • Homepage feed solves the "what's this site for" problem

Cons

  • More design work - each zone needs its own treatment
  • Risk of feeling disjointed if not carefully unified

C: The Rogue Option

Dark, warm, cabinet-of-curiosities. Flip to a dark background with warm cream text. The PDIA images become the star - displayed prominently as full decorative elements, not just accents. Gold highlights on dark feels like a museum vitrine or a letterpress print. Same Charter serif, same soul, completely different mood. The "aged paper" becomes "aged cabinet".

Pros

  • Dramatic transformation that still feels like you
  • PDIA images look stunning on dark backgrounds
  • Photos pop against dark, books/films feel curated

Cons

  • Dark sites can feel heavy for long-form reading
  • Bigger departure - might miss the warmth
  • Needs careful contrast work for accessibility
